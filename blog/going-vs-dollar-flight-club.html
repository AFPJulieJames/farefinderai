TL;DR

Going and Dollar Flight Club are both flight-deal alert memberships, and they work the same way: pick your home airports, and they email you cheap fares and mistake fares. Going (formerly Scott's Cheap Flights) is known for polished international deals and a strong free tier; Dollar Flight Club competes on price with frequent discounts on its paid plan. Both have a free option, and neither is required to fly cheap, but the curation can be worth it for flexible travelers.

These two are the biggest names in the deal-alert club world, and they are genuinely similar. Choosing between them comes down to your home airports, how much you value polish, and what you are willing to pay. Here is the honest comparison.

When to choose Going

Going is the pick if you value a clean, well-curated feed and especially strong international economy deals. The free tier is genuinely useful; the paid tiers add faster alerts plus award and premium-cabin finds, which suit travelers chasing business-class mistake fares.

When to choose Dollar Flight Club

Dollar Flight Club leans into aggressive pricing and frequent promotions on its paid plan, so bargain hunters who do not mind a higher volume of alerts often find it a better value. Like Going, it lets you set multiple home airports.

The verdict

They are more alike than different; pick by home-airport coverage and whichever free trial surfaces better deals for your routes. And neither is required: a free fare comparison plus a home-airport price alert covers most trips. Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between Going and Dollar Flight Club?
Both are flight-deal alert memberships that email cheap fares from your home airports. Going is known for polished international deals and a strong free tier; Dollar Flight Club competes on aggressive pricing and frequent plan discounts.
Do Going and Dollar Flight Club have free versions?
Yes, both offer a free tier with limited deals. Paid tiers add faster alerts and extras like award and premium-cabin finds.
Which is better, Going or Dollar Flight Club?
They are similar; choose by home-airport coverage and whichever free trial surfaces better deals for your routes. Going leans toward polish and quality, Dollar Flight Club toward bargain volume.
Do I need a deal-alert membership to fly cheap?
No. A free fare comparison plus a home-airport price alert covers most travelers. A paid club is a convenience for flexible, frequent flyers, not a requirement.
