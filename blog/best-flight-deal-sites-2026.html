Best Flight Deal Sites of 2026 (Ranked)

JJ
Julie James
Founder, FareFinderAI
Updated June 18, 2026 · 9 min read
TL;DR

There are two kinds of flight deal sites: alert services that email you cheap fares (Going, Thrifty Traveler, Dollar Flight Club, Jack's Flight Club, Secret Flying) and search tools you query yourself (Skyscanner, Google Flights, and comparison tools that catch hidden-city and budget fares). For most travelers, the best setup is one free alert service plus a good search tool. Paid tiers are worth it only if you fly a few times a year from a steady home airport and stay flexible on dates - one good alert pays for the year.

"Best flight deal site" depends on how you travel. If you want deals delivered to you, you need an alert service. If you need a specific trip on specific dates, you need a search tool. Here's the 2026 ranking for both, and how to combine them.

Best deal-alert services (deals come to you)

1. Going (formerly Scott's Cheap Flights) - best all-rounder

The biggest and most polished alert service. Pick your home airports and it emails cheap and mistake fares. Free tier plus Premium (around $49/year) and Elite (around $199/year, adds premium-cabin and award deals). Best for flexible travelers who want quality alerts with minimal effort.

2. Thrifty Traveler - best curation

Strong, well-explained domestic and international deals, with a paid Premium tier. The team's write-ups and point-redemption deals are a highlight. A great alternative or complement to Going.

3. Dollar Flight Club - best budget option

Free tier gives 1-3 domestic deals a week from one airport; paid plans (around $69-$99/year) add daily alerts, mistake fares, premium cabins, and more airports. Good value if you want frequent alerts cheaply.

4. Jack's Flight Club - best for UK/Europe departures

A long-running service with a sharp sense of humor and strong European coverage. Free and Premium tiers. Worth it if you fly transatlantic or from Europe.

5. Secret Flying & The Flight Deal - best fully free feeds

These publish error fares and deals publicly (web and social) at no cost. No personalization, so you'll scroll past a lot of irrelevant routes, but the price is unbeatable and they catch genuine mistake fares fast.

Best search tools (you go find the deal)

Alert services are passive. When you have a specific trip, you need to search. The free engines - Google Flights, Skyscanner, Kayak - are the starting point (compared in detail in our Skyscanner vs Google Flights vs Kayak guide). But they share a blind spot: none shows hidden-city or most split-ticket fares, and each omits some carriers. That's where comparison tools come in - our flight finder tool comparison ranks the ones that catch what the big engines miss, led by Aviasales for agency fares and Kiwi for hidden-city routings.

The setup we recommend

Don't choose one - layer them:

  • One free alert service (Going or Dollar Flight Club) so opportunistic deals find you.
  • A search tool for specific trips - cross-check the free engines against a comparison tool before booking.
  • Set price alerts on your must-go routes (see our flight deal alerts guide).

Frequently asked questions

What is the best flight deal site in 2026?
For alerts, Going is the best all-rounder, with Thrifty Traveler and Dollar Flight Club close behind. For searching a specific route, use a comparison tool that includes budget and hidden-city fares. Most travelers combine both.
Are paid flight deal sites worth it?
If you fly a few times a year from a steady home airport and stay flexible, one good alert usually pays for the year. For fixed dates and destinations, a free search tool is often enough.
What is the best free flight deal site?
The free tiers of Going and Dollar Flight Club, free engines like Skyscanner and Google Flights, free comparison tools, and public feeds like Secret Flying and The Flight Deal.

Bottom line: pair a free alert service with a strong search tool, reserve paid tiers for frequent flexible travelers, and always cross-check before you book.
