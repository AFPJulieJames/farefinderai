TL;DR

Vietnam is one of 2026's emerging value destinations, with new capacity and competition making it cheaper to reach from the US. Fly into Ho Chi Minh City (SGN) in the south or Hanoi (HAN) in the north, usually with one stop through an Asian hub. West Coast hubs are the cheapest departure points. Go in the shoulder months and consider an open-jaw (in to one city, out the other) to travel the country without backtracking.

Vietnam keeps climbing the value lists as more airlines compete for US-to-Asia traffic, and it is famously inexpensive once you arrive. The flight is long, but very beatable with the right plan. Here is how.

The cheapest time to fly to Vietnam

Shoulder months tend to offer the best mix of fares and weather; aim outside the Western winter-holiday surge and the Lunar New Year (Tet) period, when both demand and prices climb. Because the country spans several climate zones, the open-jaw approach also lets you chase the best weather north to south.

Frequently asked questions

When is the cheapest time to fly to Vietnam?
The shoulder months, avoiding the Western winter-holiday surge and the Lunar New Year (Tet) period when demand and prices climb.
Should I fly into Hanoi or Ho Chi Minh City?
Either works. If you plan to travel the length of the country, use an open-jaw ticket, flying into one city and home from the other to avoid backtracking.
Which US cities have the cheapest flights to Vietnam?
West Coast hubs like Los Angeles, San Francisco, and Seattle have the most transpacific competition and the lowest fares, usually with one stop in Asia.
Is Vietnam cheap to visit?
Yes. It is consistently one of the most affordable countries in Southeast Asia once you arrive, and 2026 airline competition has made reaching it cheaper too.
Once the flight is booked

The three things that actually cost you money next

The fare is usually the part people optimise hardest and the part they save least on. Roaming, airport transfers and car hire are where trips quietly leak money, and all three are cheaper if you sort them before you fly.

📱

Get an eSIM before you land

Carrier roaming runs $10 to $15 a day. A local data eSIM is usually a few dollars for the whole trip, installs before you leave, and works the moment you land. This is the single easiest saving on this list.

Book the airport transfer

Airport taxi ranks are where tired travellers get overcharged, especially arriving late or without the language. A pre-booked transfer is a fixed price agreed before you fly, with a driver waiting.

🚗

Hire a car, if you're leaving the city

Worth it if the trip involves coast, countryside or day trips. Skip it if you're staying in a city with decent transit, where a car is mostly a parking problem. Book early: airport desks are the most expensive way to do this.
