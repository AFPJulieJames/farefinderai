Cheap Flights to Brazil from the US

JJ
Julie James
Founder, FareFinderAI
Updated July 20, 2026 · 8 min read
TL;DR

Brazil is cheapest from March to June and from August to early November, which is the local autumn and winter. The two windows that wreck a budget are Carnival (February or early March, depending on the year) and mid December through January. Miami is the dominant US gateway and usually the cheapest, with New York, Atlanta, Houston, Dallas and Orlando close behind. Land in Sao Paulo (GRU) for the lowest fare, Rio (GIG) if Rio is the trip, and budget separately for domestic flights, because Brazil is roughly the size of the continental US.

Brazil is one of the few genuinely long haul destinations that stays affordable for US travelers, mostly because it sits on a north to south axis rather than across an ocean. Flying overnight from Miami puts you in Sao Paulo by morning with no meaningful jet lag, since the time difference is only a couple of hours. The catch is that Brazil's fare calendar is driven by Brazilian demand, not American demand, and if you do not know the local peaks you will book straight into them.

When are flights to Brazil cheapest?

Brazil's seasons are inverted from ours. The expensive months are their summer, which means mid December through January plus Carnival. Carnival moves with the church calendar and lands in February or early March depending on the year, so check the date before you set your travel window. Fares into Rio during Carnival week can behave like a completely different route.

The value stretch is March through June and August through early November. The weather in the southeast is drier and cooler, the beaches are quieter, and pricing is at its softest. July is a mid tier month because Brazilian school holidays fall then.

Should you fly into Sao Paulo or Rio de Janeiro?

Sao Paulo Guarulhos (GRU) is Brazil's principal long haul gateway. It has the most US nonstops, the most competition, and almost always the lowest fare. If your itinerary is flexible, start here.

Rio de Janeiro Galeao (GIG) usually carries a modest premium over Sao Paulo. If Rio is actually where you are going, that premium is often less than the cost and hassle of a connecting domestic flight, so do not reflexively route through GRU to save on the international leg.

Recife (REC), Fortaleza (FOR) and Salvador (SSA) in the northeast are geographically closer to the US and sometimes surprise on price, especially from Miami. They are the right entry point for the northeast coast and worth pricing on their own rather than assuming they track Sao Paulo.

Which US gateways price lowest?

Miami is the Brazil hub for the United States, full stop. It has the most departures, the most airlines competing, and consistently the cheapest fares. New York, Atlanta, Houston, Dallas, Orlando and Los Angeles all have nonstop service depending on season and carrier.

If you do not live near one of those, price the trip both ways: as a single through ticket from your home airport, and as a cheap domestic ticket to Miami plus a separate international ticket. The second option wins more often than most travelers expect. That is a positioning flight, and the risk management around stacking two unconnected tickets is covered in split ticket flights. Leave several hours of buffer at minimum, because the two tickets are not protected together. Our cheap flights to Miami page is a reasonable place to start the domestic leg.

How far ahead should you book?

Three to six months covers most Brazil trips. Stretch to eight months or more for Carnival and for late December departures, when the cheapest fare buckets vanish first and never really return.

Brazil sits in a useful middle ground: it has more frequency than the Australia or South Africa routes, so it is more forgiving than ultra long haul, but it has far less capacity than the transatlantic market, so it is less forgiving than Europe. The practical implication is that waiting for a last minute drop is a bad strategy here. Set an alert instead, using this walkthrough for flight price alerts, and read how far in advance to book international flights for the general framework. Midweek departures still help, as covered in cheapest days to fly.

How do you handle Brazil's domestic distances?

This is where most first time budgets break. Brazil is roughly the size of the continental United States, and the distances are not intuitive from a map. Sao Paulo to Salvador is a genuine flight, not a bus ride. Manaus and the Amazon are another long hop again.

  • Book domestic legs separately from the international ticket. Brazilian carriers frequently price their own routes below what the international airline charges to tack the leg on.
  • Fly an open jaw. Into Sao Paulo, out of Rio or Recife, avoids paying twice to cross the country. See open jaw flights.
  • Watch the baggage rules. Domestic carry on and checked allowances often differ from what your international ticket included. Our guide to avoiding airline baggage fees applies here.
  • Leave real connection time. Guarulhos immigration and customs can be slow at peak arrival banks, and a separately booked domestic flight will not wait for you.

What about entry requirements?

Brazil's entry rules for US citizens have changed more than once in recent years, including the visa requirement being dropped and later reinstated. Do not trust a blog post on this, including ours. Check the US State Department country page and the Brazilian consulate site for your region before you book, and check again a few weeks before you fly. If a visa or authorization is required, the processing time can be long enough to affect when you book, so make it the first thing you confirm rather than the last.

Frequently asked questions

When are flights to Brazil cheapest?
March through June and August through early November, which is Brazil's autumn and winter. Avoid Carnival, which falls in February or early March depending on the year, and mid December through January, the peak of Brazilian summer holidays.
Should I fly into Sao Paulo or Rio de Janeiro?
Sao Paulo Guarulhos is the main long haul gateway and almost always cheaper, with more US nonstops. Rio Galeao carries a small premium that is often worth paying if Rio is your destination. Compare both, and consider flying into one and out of the other.
Which US cities have nonstop flights to Brazil?
Miami dominates and is usually cheapest, with New York, Atlanta, Houston, Dallas, Orlando and Los Angeles also offering nonstops depending on the season. From other cities, a separate ticket to Miami plus a separate international ticket often beats the through fare.
How far in advance should I book flights to Brazil?
Three to six months for most trips, and eight months or more for Carnival and late December, when cheap fare buckets sell out early and do not come back.

Brazil is one of the best value long haul trips available from the US, provided you dodge two predictable peaks and treat the domestic flying as a separate budget line. Pick an autumn or winter month, start from Miami if you reasonably can, and set your alert early.
