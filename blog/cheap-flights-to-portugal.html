TL;DR

Portugal is one of the most affordable doorways into Western Europe, with Lisbon often among the cheapest transatlantic fares from the US East Coast. Fly into Lisbon (LIS) for the best connections and prices, or Porto (OPO) for the north. Go in the spring or fall shoulder season to dodge the summer peak, when European fares run about 20% higher in 2026, and use Lisbon as a cheap launch point for the rest of Europe.

Lisbon has quietly become one of the best-value gateways to Europe, with strong competition from US carriers and Portugal's own airline keeping fares down. Here is how to take advantage, and how to use Portugal as a cheap launchpad for a wider trip.

How to find cheap flights to Portugal

East Coast hubs like New York, Boston, and Newark have the most nonstops to Lisbon and the lowest fares, since Portugal is one of the closer European mainland destinations. From there, budget carriers fan out across Europe cheaply, so Lisbon doubles as a split-ticket entry point.

The cheapest time to fly to Portugal

Spring (April to May) and fall (September to October) are the best value and arguably the nicest weather, avoiding both the summer crowds and the summer price peak. Summer fares to Europe are up about 20% in 2026, so if you must go then, book 4 to 6 months out per how far ahead to book international flights.

How to book it cheap

  • Fly from an East Coast hub for the most nonstops and lowest fares.
  • Use Lisbon as a launchpad and continue on a budget carrier with a open-jaw home leg.

Frequently asked questions

When is the cheapest time to fly to Portugal?
Spring (April to May) and fall (September to October) are the best value. Summer is the crowded, expensive peak, with 2026 European fares up about 20%.
What is the cheapest airport to fly into in Portugal?
Lisbon (LIS) has the most US nonstops and the lowest fares. Porto (OPO) is better for the north but often requires a European connection.
Why is Portugal a cheap way to reach Europe?
Lisbon is one of the closer European mainland gateways, with strong US-carrier and Portuguese-airline competition, so it frequently has among the lowest transatlantic fares, and budget carriers connect onward cheaply.
Which US cities have the cheapest flights to Portugal?
East Coast hubs, especially New York, Boston, and Newark, have the most nonstops and the lowest fares to Lisbon.
