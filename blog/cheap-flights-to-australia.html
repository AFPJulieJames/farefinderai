Cheap Flights to Australia from the US

JJ
Julie James
Founder, FareFinderAI
Updated July 20, 2026 · 8 min read
TL;DR

Flights to Australia are cheapest in the southern shoulder seasons: late April to early June, and September to early November. Fly from a West Coast gateway if you can, because Los Angeles and San Francisco carry the most nonstop competition and price lowest. Book four to seven months out (eight to ten for Christmas), compare Sydney against Melbourne and Brisbane every time, and consider an open jaw so you do not fly the country twice. The single most expensive mistake is booking for mid December to January, when Australian school holidays and the Northern Hemisphere winter escape collide.

Australia is one of the longest hauls a US traveler can take, and long haul routes behave differently from short ones. There are fewer flights, fewer airlines, and far less cheap seat inventory per departure. That combination punishes late bookers and rewards anyone willing to move their dates by a few weeks. Here is what actually moves the price.

When are flights to Australia cheapest?

Australia's seasons are inverted from ours, and its fare calendar follows local demand more than American demand. The peak is mid December through January, when Australian summer holidays run and Americans are looking to escape winter. That is the window to avoid.

The value windows are the two shoulders. Late April to early June lands after the Easter rush and before the ski and school breaks. September to early November is spring in Australia: mild weather, wildflowers in the west, and fares that often sit well below the summer peak. Which Australian airport should you fly into?

Sydney (SYD) carries the most nonstop service from the United States and is the default cheapest arrival point for most travelers. Melbourne (MEL) is usually within a few percent and sometimes undercuts Sydney outright, so it deserves a search every single time. Brisbane (BNE) has grown its nonstop network and is the smart entry point for the Great Barrier Reef and the Gold Coast.

Perth (PER) is a different animal. It is on the wrong side of a very large continent, so US itineraries almost always route through an Asian or Middle Eastern hub, or through the east coast. Price it separately rather than assuming it will track Sydney.

Which US gateways give the cheapest Australia fares?

Pacific routes concentrate at a handful of West Coast airports, and that competition is what sets the floor. Los Angeles (LAX) and San Francisco (SFO) are the two to beat. Dallas (DFW), Houston (IAH), Seattle (SEA) and Honolulu (HNL) are all worth checking depending on your home airport.

If you live on the East Coast, do not assume the through fare from your city is the best deal. Buying a cheap domestic ticket to LAX or SFO and a separate international ticket can undercut the single itinerary by a wide margin. That is a positioning flight, and the mechanics of stacking two separate tickets are covered in split ticket flights. Just leave yourself a generous buffer, ideally an overnight, because separate tickets are not protected together.

How far ahead should you book?

Four to seven months is the working range for most Australia trips. Push to eight to ten months for departures between mid December and mid January.

The reason is inventory, not luck. A single daily widebody to Sydney might hold only a handful of seats in the cheapest fare buckets. Once those sell, the price steps up and does not usually come back down. That is why the last minute discounting you sometimes see on domestic routes essentially does not exist on the Pacific. What routing tricks work on Australia flights?

  • Fly an open jaw. Into Sydney, out of Melbourne or Brisbane, saves a domestic backtrack and often costs nothing extra. See open jaw flights.
  • Consider a free stopover. Several carriers route the Pacific via Asian hubs, and some let you break the trip for a few days at no fare penalty. It turns one long haul into two trips.
  • Check Honolulu as a break point. Splitting the journey at HNL is sometimes cheaper than the through fare and makes the flying far more humane.
  • Read the bag rules before you celebrate. Long haul basic economy exists and can strip checked bags and seat selection. Our take is in is basic economy worth it and how to avoid airline baggage fees.

How do you handle the flight itself?

The Pacific crossing runs roughly 14 to 17 hours depending on gateway and direction. Almost all US departures leave in the evening and arrive two calendar days later, which is a scheduling quirk rather than a lost day. Coming home, you typically land the same day you left.

Practical notes: westbound is the harder direction for most people, so protect sleep on the outbound and plan a low key first day. Frequently asked questions

When are flights to Australia cheapest?
In the shoulder seasons: roughly late April through early June, and September through early November. Avoid mid December through January, which is the Australian summer holiday peak and the most expensive window of the year.
Which US airports have the cheapest flights to Australia?
West Coast gateways, because they carry the most nonstop competition. Los Angeles and San Francisco usually price lowest, with Dallas, Houston, Seattle and Honolulu also worth comparing. From the East Coast, a separate positioning flight to the West Coast is often cheaper than a through fare.
How far in advance should I book flights to Australia?
Four to seven months for most trips, and eight to ten months for Christmas and New Year travel. Ultra long haul Pacific routes carry very little cheap seat inventory, so the lowest buckets sell out earlier than on transatlantic routes.
Should I fly into Sydney or Melbourne?
Sydney has the most nonstop service and is usually the default cheapest option, but Melbourne is often within a few percent and sometimes cheaper. Compare both, and consider an open jaw that flies into one and out of the other so you do not backtrack.

Australia rewards planning more than almost any other destination, simply because the cheap seats are so scarce. Pick a shoulder month, stay flexible on which east coast city you land in, and start watching fares early. The gap between a well timed booking and a panicked one is usually the largest single cost in the whole trip.
