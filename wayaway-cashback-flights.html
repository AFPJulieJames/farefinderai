What is WayAway?

WayAway: The Flight Search That Pays You Back

WayAway is a flight aggregator built by the same team behind Aviasales — with one key twist: it pays cashback on bookings made through the platform. Instead of just comparing prices and sending you to book elsewhere, WayAway earns affiliate commissions from airlines and agencies, then shares a portion of that commission with you as real, withdrawable cashback.

The free version of WayAway lets you search and compare flights without any cashback benefit. The WayAway Plus membership ($49.99/year or $7.99/month) unlocks cashback of up to 10% on flights and up to 10% on hotel bookings made through the platform. For frequent travelers, a single booking can easily offset the annual membership fee.

WayAway is not a replacement for Aviasales or Kiwi — it doesn't find as broad a range of routes or fare combinations. But as a layer on top of your normal booking process, it's one of the most straightforward ways to reduce your net travel cost without changing how or when you fly.

How it works

How WayAway Cashback Actually Works

The cashback model is straightforward — here's the process from search to payout.

🔍
Step 01

Search on WayAway

Search for your flight on WayAway exactly as you would on any other aggregator. WayAway compares prices across airlines and agencies and shows you the best available fares.

🎫
Step 02

Book Through the Platform

When you click through and book your flight on WayAway Plus, the platform earns an affiliate commission from the airline or booking agency. This is the standard commission model all aggregators use — WayAway just shares it with you.

💰
Step 03

Earn Your Cashback

WayAway credits cashback to your account after the booking is confirmed. Cashback rates vary by route and partner — typically 1–5% on flights and up to 10% on hotels. Cashback accumulates in your WayAway account.

💳
Step 04

Withdraw Real Money

Once you reach the minimum withdrawal threshold, you can transfer your cashback as real money to PayPal or your bank account. It's not points or credits — it's actual withdrawable cash.

Pro tips

Getting the Most from WayAway Cashback

How to maximize your cashback and decide if WayAway Plus is the right call for your travel habits.

Tip 01

Stack WayAway with the Best Fare Search

Find the cheapest fare first on Aviasales or Kiwi, then search the same route on WayAway to see if the price is comparable. If it is, book on WayAway to earn cashback on the same price you'd pay elsewhere.

Tip 02

Hotels Give Better Cashback Rates

WayAway's hotel cashback rates are typically higher than flight cashback rates. If you book hotels through WayAway regularly, the Plus membership pays for itself faster — even if your flight spend is modest.

Tip 03

Try the Monthly Membership First

WayAway Plus is available as a monthly subscription at $7.99/month. If you have a trip coming up with significant flight and hotel spend, subscribe for one month, book everything, and evaluate whether annual membership makes sense.

Tip 04

Cashback Is Real Money, Not Points

Unlike airline miles or credit card travel points, WayAway cashback is withdrawable to PayPal or your bank account. There are no expiry dates, no blackout restrictions, and no minimum spend to maintain status.

Tip 05

Best for Frequent or High-Spend Travelers

WayAway Plus returns the most value for travelers flying 3+ times per year or booking higher-cost international routes. Budget domestic travelers on 1–2 trips per year may not see enough cashback to justify the annual fee.

Tip 06

Combine With Other Savings Strategies

WayAway cashback works on top of any fare you find — including deals from flight deal alerts and mistake fares. Finding a cheap fare and earning cashback on it simultaneously gives you the maximum possible savings on any booking.

Common questions

WayAway Cashback — FAQ

Is WayAway legit? Is the cashback real?
Yes — WayAway is a legitimate travel platform built by the Aviasales team, one of the most established names in flight search. The cashback is real money, not points or credits, and is withdrawable to PayPal or a bank account. WayAway earns standard affiliate commissions from airlines and booking partners, then shares a portion with Plus members. The model is transparent and the cashback payments are genuine.
How much cashback does WayAway actually pay?
Cashback rates vary by booking partner and route. Flight cashback typically ranges from 1% to 5% depending on the airline and agency. Hotel cashback rates are often higher — up to 10% on some properties. WayAway displays the cashback rate for each booking before you commit, so you always know exactly what you'll earn before clicking through to book.
Is WayAway Plus worth the $49.99/year fee?
For travelers taking 3 or more trips per year, or booking hotels regularly through the platform, WayAway Plus typically pays for itself and delivers net positive savings. For travelers taking 1–2 trips per year on budget domestic routes, the math is closer. The monthly plan at $7.99 is a good way to test the value before committing to an annual membership.
How does WayAway compare to Aviasales?
Aviasales and WayAway are built by the same company and share similar underlying technology. Aviasales typically indexes a slightly wider range of agencies and fares. WayAway adds the cashback layer on top. For raw fare comparison, Aviasales is our top pick. For travelers who prioritize earning cashback on their bookings and fly frequently enough to benefit from Plus, WayAway is a valuable complement — not a replacement — to Aviasales.
When does cashback get credited and how do I withdraw it?
Cashback is typically credited to your WayAway account after the travel date has passed and the booking is confirmed as completed. This protects against cancellations. Once credited, you can withdraw to PayPal or a bank account when you reach the minimum withdrawal threshold. The exact timing varies by booking partner — WayAway shows estimated credit timelines for each booking.
