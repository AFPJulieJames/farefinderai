Flight hacking tips

How to Find Cheaper Flights Every Time

The strategies frequent travelers use to pay less on every booking -- regardless of where they fly.

Tip 01

Search in Incognito Mode

Flight prices can increase when booking sites detect you have searched the same route multiple times. Always search for cheap flights in a private or incognito browser window to see the lowest available fares.

Tip 02

Be Flexible on Departure Airports

Flying from a nearby airport instead of your closest one can save hundreds of dollars. Kiwi's flexible route search automatically compares nearby airports to find hidden city fares you would never discover manually.

Tip 03

Book Tuesday and Wednesday Flights

Flights departing on Tuesday and Wednesday are consistently cheaper than weekend flights. If your schedule allows any flexibility on travel days, mid-week departures are one of the easiest ways to find cheaper airfare.

Tip 04

Use Kiwi for Split Ticket Savings

Kiwi's virtual interlining technology combines tickets from multiple airlines into one cheaper itinerary. For long haul routes especially, splitting your journey across carriers can save significantly more than booking a single through ticket.

Tip 05

Always Buy Travel Insurance Before You Fly

EKTA travel insurance policies are issued within minutes and accepted by embassies worldwide for visa applications. At a fraction of the cost of a medical emergency abroad, travel insurance is the smartest money you spend on any trip.

Tip 06

Bundle Flights and Hotels on Trip.com

Trip.com's bundle pricing gives you exclusive discounts when you book flights and hotels together. For international trips especially, bundling can save significantly more than booking each component separately.
