Why one tool isn't enough

Why You're Overpaying If You Only Use One Search Engine

Every flight search engine accesses different data, uses different algorithms, and covers different airlines. Google Flights and Kayak are convenient — but they miss entire categories of fares, including virtual interlining combinations, many low-cost carrier fares, and the hidden city routes that consistently offer the lowest prices on popular routes. Using one tool is like checking one store before paying full price everywhere else. The travelers consistently paying the least check multiple tools in about five minutes — and the difference is often $100–$300 per ticket.

How to Use Cheap Flights Finders Like a Pro

The search habits that consistently deliver the lowest fares on any route.

Tip 01

Always Search Incognito

Flight prices can increase when booking sites detect repeated searches for the same route. Always use a private or incognito browser window to see the true lowest fares available.

Tip 02

Start with the Pricing Calendar

Before committing to travel dates, use Aviasales' pricing calendar to see the cheapest days to fly over a full month. A one or two day shift often saves $100–$300.

Tip 03

Run All Four Tools in Parallel

Open Aviasales, Kiwi, Trip.com, and Expedia in separate tabs and search the same route simultaneously. The five minutes it takes to compare all four consistently reveals savings that a single-tool search misses.

Tip 04

Check Nearby Airports

Always enable nearby airport search on every cheap flights finder you use. Flying from an airport 30–60 minutes away frequently saves $50–$200 — especially on one-way bookings.

Tip 05

Book Tuesday and Wednesday Departures

Mid-week departures are consistently the cheapest days to fly on most routes. If your schedule allows any flexibility, shifting departure to Tuesday or Wednesday delivers reliable savings.

Tip 06

Add Advanced Strategies on Top

Once you've found your best base fare, layer in advanced strategies: hidden city ticketing, mistake fare hunting, and fare price alerts can push savings even further.
