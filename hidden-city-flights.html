What is hidden city ticketing?

When Flying Further Actually Costs Less

Hidden city ticketing is a flight booking strategy where you book a ticket to a connecting city as your final destination — instead of flying all the way to where the ticket says you're going.

It sounds counterintuitive, but airlines price routes in ways that create these gaps. A direct flight from New York to Dallas might cost $350, while a connecting flight from New York to Phoenix — with a layover in Dallas — costs only $180. If Dallas is where you actually want to go, you simply get off at the layover and never board the second flight.

The airline doesn't benefit from telling you this. Search engines like Google Flights won't show you it. But the savings are very real — often $200 to $500 per ticket on popular domestic routes.

The key rule: Hidden city ticketing only works on one-way tickets. Never check a bag (it'll go to the final destination), and never do this on a return leg of a round trip.

How to Book a Hidden City Flight

The process is simpler than it sounds. Here's exactly what to do.

Step 02

Look for Layover Deals

Filter results to find connecting flights with a layover in your real destination. The final leg is the one you'll skip — so make sure the layover city is where you actually want to end up.

🧳
Step 03

Only Bring Carry-On Luggage

This is critical. Never check a bag on a hidden city ticket — checked bags travel to the final ticketed destination, not your layover city. Pack everything in a carry-on and personal item only.

🎫
Step 04

Book as One-Way Only

Hidden city ticketing only works on one-way bookings. If you use it on the outbound leg of a round trip, the airline may cancel your return flight. Always book one-way for any hidden city fare.

🚪
Step 05

Exit at the Layover

When you land at your real destination (the layover city), simply collect your carry-on and walk out of the airport. You don't board the connecting flight to the final ticketed destination.

💰
Step 06

Keep the Savings

That's it. The fare difference is yours to keep. On popular domestic routes, hidden city ticketing regularly saves travelers $100 to $500 per ticket — sometimes more on international routes.

Search Early and Set Price Alerts

Hidden city fare gaps are most pronounced 3–6 weeks before departure. Search early and use Kiwi's price alerts to track when a good combination appears for your route.

Tip 02

Hub Cities Create the Most Opportunities

Major airline hubs — Dallas, Chicago, Atlanta, Phoenix, Denver — show up as layover cities most often. If you live near or want to visit a hub, hidden city fares are especially common.

Common questions

Hidden City Flights — FAQ

Is hidden city ticketing legal?
Yes — hidden city ticketing is legal for passengers. It does violate most airlines' terms of service, meaning they can theoretically revoke your frequent flyer miles or close your account if you do it repeatedly and are flagged. But it carries no legal risk for travelers. The practice is entirely on the passenger side: you bought a ticket, you flew a portion of the route. Doing it occasionally and sensibly (no checked bags, no miles redemptions, booking one-way) carries minimal practical risk.
Will the airline cancel my seat if I don't show up for the final leg?
On a one-way ticket, no — the journey is complete once you've used the leg you needed. The risk of automatic cancellation applies to round trips: if you no-show on the final outbound segment, some airlines will cancel your return flight. This is exactly why hidden city ticketing must always be done on one-way bookings only.
Can I check a bag on a hidden city flight?
No. Checked bags are automatically routed to the final ticketed destination — not the layover city where you exit. If you check a bag on a hidden city ticket, it will continue to the final city without you. Always travel carry-on only when using this strategy.
Does Kiwi.com find hidden city flights automatically?
Yes. Kiwi's virtual interlining technology automatically evaluates thousands of route combinations — including itineraries where the layover city is cheaper to route through than to fly to directly. It surfaces these combinations alongside regular results, so you can compare and choose. It's the most powerful tool available for finding hidden city fares without manually piecing together routes yourself.
How much can I realistically save with hidden city flights?
Savings vary significantly by route, date, and timing. On popular domestic routes in the US — especially those connecting through major hubs — hidden city fares regularly save $100 to $400 per ticket. On some international routes with hub connections, the gap can be even larger. The savings are most pronounced on routes where direct flights are expensive because of limited competition, but connecting itineraries involve more competitive markets.
What's the difference between hidden city flights and mistake fares?
Hidden city ticketing is a deliberate routing strategy — you're booking a ticket to a farther destination and exiting at the layover. Mistake fares are pricing errors by airlines or booking agencies where a flight is accidentally listed far below its intended price.
